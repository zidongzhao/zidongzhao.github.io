About me

 

I am a Research Scientist at Yale University's Science of Well-being initiative, where I collaborate with Laurie Santos and an interdisciplinary team to enhance human flourishing through large scale online courses and data-driven approaches. In my work, I leverage advanced statistical modeling, machine learning, surveys, and experimentation to transform complex behavioral data from millions of users into actionable insights for product design, teaching practices, and cross-functional decision making.

Previously, I completed my doctoral training in the Princeton Social Neuroscience Lab, where I worked primarily with Diana Tamir and Tom Griffiths, and learnt enormously from fabulous mentors and collaborators. I earned my BA from Vanderbilt University, where I did clinical psychology research with Bunmi Olatunji and Sohee Park. You can learn more about my academic work through my research and publciations.
